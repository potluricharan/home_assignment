URANUS

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is known for its blue-green color caused by methane gas in its atmosphere.

    Key facts about Uranus:
  • Seventh planet from the Sun
  • Ice giant planet
  • Rotates on its side
  • Has 27 known moons
  • Has faint ring system

Uranus has a diameter of about 50,724 km. It rotates in a unique sideways direction compared to other planets. Uranus is one of the coldest planets in the solar system.

Uranus has several internal layers.

    Main layers of Uranus:
  1. Atmosphere
  2. Icy mantle
  3. Rocky core

Uranus has a cold and thick atmosphere.

    Main components of the atmosphere::
  1. Hydrogen
  2. Helium
  3. Methane

Uranus has a system of faint rings and several moons. Its unusual sideways rotation causes extreme seasons that can last for many years. Scientists continue studying Uranus to learn more about ice giant planets.
