MARS

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is often called the Red Planet because of its reddish appearance. The red color comes from iron oxide, or rust, on its surface. Mars is a cold desert world with a thin atmosphere, and scientists believe that water once existed on this planet.

    Key facts about Mars:
  • Fourth planet from the Sun
  • Known as the Red Planet
  • Has two moons: Phobos and Deimos
  • Home to the largest volcano in the solar system
  • Surface contains valleys, deserts, and polar ice caps

Mars has a diameter of about 6,779 km, making it about half the size of Earth. The planet has a thin atmosphere and a cold climate. Mars experiences large dust storms that can cover the entire planet. Mars has a day length similar to Earth, lasting about 24.6 hours. However, a year on Mars is much longer, taking about 687 Earth days to complete one orbit around the Sun. The surface of Mars contains many geological features including mountains, valleys, and impact craters. Olympus Mons, the tallest volcano in the solar system, is located on Mars.

Mars is made up of several internal layers similar to other rocky planets. These layers help scientists understand the planet’s geological history and internal structure.

    Main layers of Mars:
  1. Crust – Rocky outer layer that forms the surface
  2. Mantle – Thick middle layer made of silicate rock
  3. Core – Center of the planet made mostly of iron, nickel, and sulfur

Mars has a thin atmosphere that cannot hold much heat, which is why the planet is very cold compared to Earth.

    Main components of the atmosphere::
  1. Carbon dioxide – 95%
  2. Nitrogen – 2.7%
  3. Argon – 1.6%

Many spacecraft and artificial satellites have been sent to orbit Mars to study its surface and atmosphere. Scientists use these satellites to take detailed images, analyze the atmosphere, and search for signs of past water on the planet.

Mars missions help researchers understand the planet's climate, geology, and possibility of past life.

Examples of Mars Satellites and Missions

  • Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter – Studies the surface and climate of Mars
  • Mars Odyssey – One of the longest-operating spacecraft around Mars
  • Mars Express – European spacecraft studying Mars geology
  • Mangalyaan (Mars Orbiter Mission) – India's first mission to Mars
  • ExoMars Trace Gas Orbiter – Studies gases in the Martian atmosphere
