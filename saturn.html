SATURN

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is famous for its beautiful rings.

    Key facts about Saturn:
  • Sixth planet
  • Second largest planet
  • Gas giant
  • More than 80 moons
  • Famous ring system

Saturn has a diameter of about 120,536 km. Its rings are made of ice and rock particles. Saturn rotates quickly and has strong winds.

Saturn has several internal layers.

    Main layers of Saturn:
  1. Cloud layer
  2. Hydrogen layer
  3. Metallic hydrogen
  4. Core

Saturn has a thick atmosphere.

    Main components of the atmosphere::
  1. Hydrogen
  2. Helium
  3. Methane

Saturn is best known for its spectacular ring system, which is made of billions of ice and rock particles. These rings orbit around the planet and can stretch thousands of kilometers across but are very thin. Saturn also has many natural satellites (moons). The largest moon, Titan, is even bigger than the planet Mercury and has a thick atmosphere. Scientists study Saturn’s rings and moons to better understand how planets and solar systems form.
