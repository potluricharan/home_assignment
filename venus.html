VENUS

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is often called Earth's twin because it is similar in size and structure. However, Venus is the hottest planet in the solar system due to its thick atmosphere that traps heat.

    Key facts about Venus:
  • Second planet from the Sun
  • Similar size to Earth
  • Hottest planet in the solar system
  • Covered with thick clouds
  • No natural satellites

Venus has a diameter of about 12,104 km. The planet rotates very slowly and in the opposite direction compared to most planets. A day on Venus is longer than its year. The surface of Venus contains volcanic plains and mountains.

Venus is made up of several internal layers similar to other rocky planets.

    Main layers of Venus:
  1. Crust – Rocky outer surface
  2. Mantle – Thick middle layer
  3. Core – Metallic central core

Venus has a very thick atmosphere.

    Main components of the atmosphere::
  1. Carbon dioxide
  2. Nitrogen
  3. Sulfur dioxide
