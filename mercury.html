MERCURY

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest planet in the solar system. It has a rocky surface filled with many craters caused by meteor impacts. Because Mercury is very close to the Sun, it experiences extreme temperatures.

    Key facts about Mercury:
  • Closest planet to the Sun
  • Smallest planet in the solar system
  • No natural satellites
  • One year lasts 88 Earth days
  • Surface covered with craters

Mercury has a diameter of about 4,879 km. It rotates slowly and has almost no atmosphere to trap heat. During the day the temperature can reach about 430°C, while at night it can drop to -180°C. Mercury’s surface is similar to the Moon with many impact craters and rocky plains.

Mercury is made up of several internal layers similar to other rocky planets.

    Main layers of Mercury:
  1. Crust – Thin rocky outer layer
  2. Mantle – Middle rocky layer
  3. Core – Large iron core at the center

Mercury has a very thin atmosphere called an exosphere.

    Main components of the atmosphere::
  1. Oxygen
  2. Sodium
  3. Hydrogen
