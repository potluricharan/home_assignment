EARTH

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only planet known to support life. It has the right temperature, atmosphere, and water that allow living organisms to survive. Earth formed about 4.5 billion years ago and continues to change through natural processes.

    Key facts about Earth:
  • Third planet from the Sun
  • About 4.5 billion years old
  • 71% of the surface is covered by water
  • Only known planet with life
  • Has one natural satellite (the Moon)

Earth's dimensions reveal it as an oblate spheroid, slightly flattened at the poles due to rotation. Its equatorial diameter measures 12,756 km (7,926 miles), while the polar diameter is 12,714 km (7,900 miles), creating a 42 km difference. The equatorial circumference spans 40,075 km (24,901 miles), compared to the polar circumference of 40,008 km (24,860 miles). Earth's total volume reaches 1.086 trillion cubic km, with a mass of 5.97 × 10²⁴ kg and average density of 5,517 kg/m³. Its surface area totals 510 million square km, where oceans cover 71% and land occupies 29%

Earth is made up of several layers, each with different materials and temperatures. These layers help scientists understand earthquakes, volcanoes, and the planet’s magnetic field..

    Main layers of Earth:
  1. Crust – The outer layer where humans live
  2. Mantle – A thick layer of hot rock beneath the crust
  3. Outer Core – A liquid layer made mostly of iron and nickel
  4. Inner Core – A solid center with extremely high temperature

The atmosphere is a protective layer of gases surrounding Earth. It protects the planet from harmful radiation from the Sun and helps maintain suitable temperatures for life.

    Main components of the atmosphere::
  1. Nitrogen-78%
  2. oxygen-21%
  3. argon-0.93%

Artificial satellites are human-made machines that orbit around Earth. Scientists launch them into space using rockets, and once they reach the correct height, they begin moving around Earth due to gravity and their high speed. These satellites travel in specific paths called orbits and help scientists observe Earth, communicate across long distances, and collect important data about weather and space.

Today, thousands of artificial satellites are moving around Earth and play an important role in modern technology and scientific research.

Types of Artificial Satellites Around Earth

  • Communication satellites – Used for television, internet, and phone signals
  • Weather satellites – Help predict storms, rainfall, and climate changes
  • Navigation satellites – Used in GPS systems for maps and location tracking
  • Earth observation satellites – Capture images of Earth for research and monitoring
  • Scientific satellites – Used for space and environmental research
