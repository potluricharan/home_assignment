JUPITER

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest planet in the solar system. It is a gas giant mainly made of hydrogen and helium.

    Key facts about Jupiter:
  • Largest planet in the solar system
  • Fifth planet from the Sun
  • Gas giant
  • More than 90 moons
  • Famous Great Red Spot storm

Jupiter has a diameter of about 143,000 km. It rotates very quickly and completes one day in about 10 hours. Jupiter has strong storms and powerful magnetic fields.

Jupiter has several internal layers.

    Main layers of Jupiter:
  1. Cloud layer
  2. Liquid hydrogen layer
  3. Metallic hydrogen layer
  4. Core

Jupiter has a thick atmosphere.

    Main components of the atmosphere::
  1. Hydrogen
  2. Helium
  3. Methane

Jupiter is famous for the Great Red Spot, a giant storm that has been active for more than 300 years. The planet also has many moons including Ganymede, the largest moon in the solar system. Scientists study Jupiter to understand gas giant planets.
